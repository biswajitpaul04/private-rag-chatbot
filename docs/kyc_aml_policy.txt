KYC AND ANTI-MONEY-LAUNDERING POLICY SUMMARY
Meridian National Bank (sample / fictional)

PURPOSE
This summary describes, in simplified form, how the bank verifies customer
identity and monitors transactions to prevent money laundering and the
financing of illicit activity. It is a fictional illustration for testing.

CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD)
Before opening any account, the bank collects and verifies:
- Full legal name and date of birth.
- A government-issued photo identity document.
- Proof of current residential address.
- The customer's stated purpose for the account and expected activity.

RISK CATEGORIES
Each customer is assigned a risk rating at onboarding:
- Low risk: salaried individuals with stable, predictable activity.
- Medium risk: self-employed customers and small businesses.
- High risk: customers with complex ownership, high cash volumes, or links
  to higher-risk jurisdictions. High-risk customers undergo Enhanced Due
  Diligence (EDD) and more frequent review.

ENHANCED DUE DILIGENCE (EDD)
For high-risk customers the bank additionally collects source-of-funds
documentation, identifies beneficial owners, and obtains senior management
approval before onboarding.

TRANSACTION MONITORING
Automated systems screen transactions against expected behavior. Patterns
that may trigger a review include:
- Multiple cash deposits just below reporting thresholds (structuring).
- Sudden high-value transfers inconsistent with the customer profile.
- Funds quickly moved through the account with no clear economic purpose.

REPORTING
Transactions that appear suspicious are escalated internally to the compliance
team, which decides whether to file a Suspicious Transaction Report (STR) with
the relevant financial intelligence authority. Employees must not inform the
customer that a report has been filed ("no tipping-off").

RECORD RETENTION
Customer identification records and transaction records are retained for a
minimum of five years after the end of the relationship, in line with
regulatory expectations.

TRAINING
All customer-facing staff complete annual AML training and an assessment.
