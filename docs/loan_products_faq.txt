RETAIL LOAN PRODUCTS - FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Meridian National Bank (sample / fictional)

Q: What types of retail loans does the bank offer?
A: The bank offers personal loans, home loans, vehicle loans, and education
   loans. Each has its own eligibility criteria, interest range, and tenure.

Q: What is the difference between a fixed and a floating interest rate?
A: A fixed rate stays the same for the agreed period, so the monthly
   instalment does not change. A floating rate moves with a reference
   benchmark, so instalments can rise or fall over time.

Q: How is a personal loan EMI calculated?
A: The Equated Monthly Instalment (EMI) depends on three things: the loan
   amount (principal), the interest rate, and the tenure in months. A longer
   tenure lowers the monthly EMI but increases the total interest paid.

PERSONAL LOAN
- Amount: 50,000 to 2,000,000 units.
- Interest: 10.5% to 16% per annum, depending on credit profile.
- Tenure: 12 to 60 months.
- Collateral: none (unsecured).

HOME LOAN
- Amount: up to 80% of property value.
- Interest: 8.4% to 9.5% per annum (floating).
- Tenure: up to 300 months.
- Collateral: the financed property is mortgaged to the bank.

VEHICLE LOAN
- Amount: up to 85% of vehicle on-road price.
- Interest: 9% to 12% per annum.
- Tenure: 12 to 84 months.
- Collateral: the vehicle is hypothecated to the bank.

Q: What is a credit score and why does it matter?
A: A credit score is a number summarizing a borrower's repayment history.
   A higher score signals lower risk and generally leads to a better interest
   rate and a higher chance of approval.

Q: What happens if an EMI is missed?
A: A late-payment fee applies and the missed payment is reported to the credit
   bureau, which can lower the borrower's credit score. Persistent default may
   lead to recovery action against any pledged collateral.

Q: Can a loan be prepaid?
A: Yes. Floating-rate home loans usually carry no prepayment charge. Fixed-rate
   and personal loans may carry a prepayment fee of up to 2% of the outstanding
   amount. Customers should check their specific loan agreement.
